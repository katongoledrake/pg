Our Stories

Mubiru Zidane

They lived 6 miles away from our home so whenever we went to the market, we would see them sitting alongside the road selling their cassava. A sad day came when an accident occurred which took the life of his mother, leaving him orphaned and homeless since he had no other living relative [MOST MOTHERS IN UGANDA LIVING IN POVERTY GIVE BIRTH TO CHILDREN AFTER SEXUAL HARASSMENT AND RAPE SO THE CHILDREN GROW UP FATHERLESS]. At first he was taken in by Mama George who was a friend of her mother to take care of him and his needs but things didn't go as he expected. Mama George got sick and told us her illness began as a small wound on her leg which itched a lot and then swelled, and then her leg started rotting

When she told us all about her and we saw she couldn't walk or feed herself or him we took her to the cancer institute in Uganda to receive free treatment given to poor people since her documents were showing that she had cancer. We took her for treatment, and he was taken to start living with us. However much we were struggling ourselves, we couldn't leave such a soul of a young boy to suffer. Mama George died at the institute in months, but until then he didn't know what death was because he sometimes asked where his mother went. He is now a happy boy surrounded by other children. He likes reciting the Bible and playing football.
