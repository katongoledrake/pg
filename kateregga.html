Our Stories

kateregga Geofrey

“The local women helped the mother in giving birth but they didn’t have more knowledge about how to handle the mother. She was already tired and bleeding too much. After like three hours, she couldn’t breathe and died.” His elder sister tells us while crying. His father was a potter who used to mold charcoal stoves for survival but could not manage to fulfill the basic needs at home and educating them. At the age of four years, with his brothers would trek three kilometers from home to the nearby trading center to sell off charcoal stoves on their heads for only UGX 2,000( 55US cents) each. To earn they had to walk an additional two kilometers to the nearest town where they would sell them off at UGX 3,500( 97US cents). Sometimes they could return back home when they haven’t sold even a single stove and the father would mistreat them for not coming home with money! He was studying in a local school but most times would miss class looking for money.

One day, together with Sister Oliver, we had travelled to their village for Gospel outreach. After impacting them with the word of God, sister Oliver asked, “ who has accepted Jesus as his or her personal savior?” He came in front and said he wants us to pray for him and his family and said he wants to talk to us. We listened to him and Sister Oliver cried hearing his story. We went to the family where they staying together with their local chairman and we rescued him! Now this is the bravest little kid I have ever met, you can take a week without hearing his voice if don’t you ask him anything but, he plays a lot almost more than anyone else, there is no way you tell him that you can’t do that because it requires training and practice, he ask to start the training right away and if you don’t keep an eye on him he injure himself in the process. He is very focused at school and prays every day so that we can be able to rescue his brothers he left in village.
